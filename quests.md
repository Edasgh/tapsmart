Great question — this is where most hackathon projects either win or fail.

👉 The mistake people make: **adding too many quests**
👉 The winning move: **focus on essential, high-impact flows**

---

# 🎯 MVP Strategy (for YOUR app)

You don’t need everything.

You need:

> ✅ “If a senior learns JUST these, they can confidently use their phone”

---

# 🏆 Perfect MVP: **5 Core Quests**

This is the **sweet spot**:

* Not too big
* Not too small
* Covers real-world usage

---

## 📱 1. Send a Message (You already built ✅)

**Why:** Most common use case

Flow:

* Open app
* Select contact
* Type message
* Send

---

## 📞 2. Make a Call

**Why:** Still the #1 feature seniors use

Flow:

* Open contacts
* Select person
* Tap call

👉 Super important for confidence

---

## 🖼️ 3. Send a Photo

**Why:** Very practical (family photos ❤️)

Flow:

* Open chat
* Tap attachment
* Select photo
* Send

---

## 🛡️ 4. Identify a Scam Message (GAME 🔥)

**Why:** Directly aligns with hackathon goal

Flow:

* Show message
* User chooses:

  * Safe / Scam
* Explain why

👉 This can be your **WOW feature**

---

## 🤖 5. Ask AI for Help

**Why:** Future-ready skill

Flow:

* Tap “Ask AI”
* Speak question
* Get simple answer

---

# 🧠 Why ONLY 5?

Because:

* Seniors get overwhelmed easily
* Judges prefer **depth over breadth**
* You can polish these REALLY well

---

# ❌ What NOT to include in MVP

Avoid:

* Settings customization
* Group chats
* Status updates
* Advanced features

👉 These dilute your focus

---

## ⚙️ 6. Adjust Volume / Brightness
